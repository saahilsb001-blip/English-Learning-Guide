1. Helping Verbs (The Trishul)

These are the 3 key words that tell us the time:

Hindi Word Time / Tense English Examples
Tha / Thi / The Past Was, Were, Had, Did
Hai / Hoon Present Is, Am, Are, Has, Have, Do
Hoga / Hogi Future Will, Will be, Will have

2. Verb Forms

The 5 states of an action word:

Form Name Example
V1 Base / Plural Present Go, Play, Eat
V2 Simple Past Went, Played, Ate
V3 Past Participle (Perfect) Gone, Played, Eaten
V4 Continuous (-ing) Going, Playing, Eating
V5 Singular Present (-s/-es) Goes, Plays, Eats

3. Regular vs Irregular

Regular Verbs: Simply add -ed for V2 and V3.
Example: Jump → Jumped → Jumped

Irregular Verbs: They change completely or don't change at all! You have to memorize these.

V1 V2 V3
Cut (No change) Cut Cut
Speak (Vowel shift) Spoke Spoken
Catch (Complete change) Caught Caught

5a. Pos / Neg / Question

The structure flips based on the sentence type. (Simple Present & Past require DO/DOES/DID for negatives!).

Type Simple Present
[+] Pos He plays cricket.
[-] Neg He does not play cricket. (V1)
[?] Que Does he play cricket?
Type Simple Past
[+] Pos He played cricket. (V2)
[-] Neg He did not play cricket. (V1)
pg. 6

5b. Pos / Neg / Question

For Continuous and Perfect, just throw "NOT" after the helping verb, and pull the helping verb to the front for questions.

Type Present Continuous
[+] Pos She is reading.
[-] Neg She is not reading.
[?] Que Is she reading?

6. Nouns & Types

A noun is a naming word. (Person, place, animal, thing, or idea).

  • Proper Noun: Specific names (Saahil, Mumbai, Honda). Always capitalized!
  • Common Noun: General names (teacher, city, car).
  • Abstract Noun: Feelings or ideas you can't touch (love, anger, freedom, time).
  • Collective Noun: A group of things (a team of players, a bunch of keys).

8. Modal Auxiliaries

Helping verbs that show "mood" or "attitude". They NEVER change form (no -s, no -ed) and are always followed by V1.

  • Can: Ability (I can swim).
  • Should: Advice (You should study).
  • Must: Obligation (You must wear a helmet).
  • May: Permission/Possibility (May I come in? It may rain).
  • Would: Polite request / Past habit (Would you help me?).
pg. 10

9. Articles Map (A, An, The)

Article Usage Rule Example
A Consonant SOUNDS A car, A university (sounds like 'y')
An Vowel SOUNDS An apple, An hour (h is silent)
The Specific / Unique things The sun, The boy in red

Rule of thumb: When you mention something for the first time, use A/An. When you talk about it again, use The!
